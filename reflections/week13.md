# Week 13 Reflection

## Reflect 13: Do we imagine AI futures as utopian, dystopian, or something in between? What do those visions reveal about us?
Not to give the go-to humanities approach to thinking about the future of AI, but I think that it could be viewed as both utopian as well as dystopian. Yesterday, I was talking to my grandmother about how she feels as though technology and the world has accelerated to the point that it has made her generation feel as though they can't "keep up." This made me think about some of what we've been discussing in this class. 


Essentially, I think that it will go in a similar way to how current technology has altered our lives. It will make certain tasks and every-day activities easier (similar to how GPS has simplified travel). However, I also believe that AI will create more problems on a personal level. While modern technology has made our lives easier, it has also opened a Pandora's box of issues, including addiction and depression.

However, I do have hope. I believe that AI can help create a future with fewer problems as long as it is implemented in a proper way. I think that this reveals that we are very uncertain today on how we implement our current technology which worries me about how we would implement future technology.
