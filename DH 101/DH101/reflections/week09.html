Week 9 Reflection

Reflect 9: What is creativity when AI can generate endlessly? Where is the human in generative work?

I would argue that when AI is able to endlessly generate, there is very little creativity. I see it as more like being a curator—sure, there is skill to be had in picking an appealing product, but it isn't necessarily an overly creative endeavor. In terms of finding the "human" in generative work, there is a multitude of ways in which our input is still used. For instance, the source material and programming in which the AI uses can be entirely curated by humans. On top of this, there is a human element in selecting what is considered to be an "acceptable" output for the AI.

In terms of building the bot, I found that through the CharacterAI tool, it was hard to "fine-tune" any of the aspects you wanted to change. While it was easy enough to make an initial input, it felt like you had to struggle to get it to do what you wanted exactly.
