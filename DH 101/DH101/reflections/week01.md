# Week 1 Reflection

## Reflect 1: What counts as "making" in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

In the age of AI, "making" doesn't just have one definition. However, by looking at Matt Ratto's definition of critical making, we can look at the emphasis of "'values' of critical design and other critical practices." One important thing to note is the lack of aesthetics. He argues that critical making doesn't follow any aesthetics but rather the goal of material exploration. In my knowledge of AI, AI seems to do something similar. It essentially creates a wealth of information without any artistic input. To me this is the biggest challenge in defining "making."

Considering that "making" is a human idea, there are many human biases behind the idea of making. For instance, even if something is made that doesn't follow any specific artistic format, it still is influenced by the perception of the one doing the "making" which would include the culture and aesthetic preferences of said person. Because of this, I believe that AI, although a valuable tool in the process of making, should not constitute as something that can "make."
