# Week 2 Reflection

## Reflect 2: Can AI help us define what it means to be human, or does it reduce us to patterns?

Overall, AI can probably be used as a tool to help define what it means to be "human." However, I feel like this would only be to a certain degree. I wouldn't go so far as to compare it with something like philosophy or art in terms of defining what it means to be "human." If we look at it from the idea of the Stochastic Parrots, AI takes information from a very large source and extracts the information without any of its "own" bias. Understanding this, artificial intelligence could be used to show human bias, however, it only grabs its data from the internet. As a result, the only "human" aspects it sees is what is shown online.

Defining what it means to be "human" is already a monumental task, but if we look at it from a personal level, AI can't understand the smaller aspects of life—it can't understand emotion, religion, art, and it essentially just repeats data that it obtains online, literally reducing aspects of life and culture to data and patterns.
