Reflection

Reflection

For my GIF, I took a clip from the 1941 educational film, How the Eye Functions. I aimed to make a GIF that creates an uneasy feeling, evoking the idea of mass surveillance in the age of AI. In doing so, I also cycled the footage back, while applying a grain effect to compress the GIF, giving it an early-internet feeling.

By repeating this footage, I aimed to create a feeling of uneasiness, giving a feeling that the viewer is being watched. After watching it multiple times as a loop, it helps amplify the uneasiness of the image, with the eyes moving as though they were watching you through the screen. If it were to be viewed only once, I believe that it wouldn’t have the same effect.

For this GIF, no generative AI was used. However, I did use the website Ezgif.com to assist with the color filtering and looping effect to transform it from a film clip to the GIF. Considering that everything that was used was originally created by a human, nearly every choice in making the GIF would have been made by a human. On the other hand, if it were to have been AI generated, it most likely wouldn’t have had the same kind of an “unsettling” feeling because of it’s struggling to represent real people like those shown in my GIF. In terms of authorship, I have mixed feelings because I essentially did what AI would have done by taking pre-existing data and remixing it with a spin. I think that there is still some credit to be given to me because I still did the work of finding and remixing the original source material myself.
